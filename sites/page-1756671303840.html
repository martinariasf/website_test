Peanuts and Uruguay

About Peanuts

Peanuts, also known as groundnuts or goobers, are a legume crop grown mainly for their edible seeds. They are an important source of protein, oil, and nutrients worldwide. Peanuts are used in various culinary applications, including snacks, peanut butter, and cooking oil.

Peanuts in Uruguay

Uruguay, a South American country known for its agriculture, cultivates peanuts as part of its diverse crop production. Though not among the top global producers, peanuts in Uruguay contribute to local consumption and some export markets.

The peanut cultivation in Uruguay benefits from its temperate climate and fertile soils, providing good conditions for quality peanut production. Efforts have been made to improve peanut varieties and agricultural practices to increase yields.

Economic and Cultural Relevance

While peanuts are not a staple in Uruguayan cuisine, they are enjoyed as snacks and used in local food products. The cultivation supports small and medium-sized farmers, contributing to rural development.
